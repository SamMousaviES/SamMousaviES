## About Me

I am a Chemical Engineering PhD researcher at Aalto University in Espoo, Finland, working on multiphase flow, liquid-liquid extraction, emulsion separation, and process modelling. My work combines CFD, population balance modelling, Aspen-based process simulation, and scientific programming to build practical engineering tools for complex fluid systems.

My background spans academic research, engineering modelling, and software development. I work mainly with OpenFOAM, Python, Matlab, C++, Aspen Plus, and Aspen Custom Modeler, and I use GitHub to share OpenFOAM developments, simulation case studies, and reproducible computational workflows.

## Current Focus

- Liquid-liquid extraction and phase separation modelling
- OpenFOAM development and custom CFD workflows
- Population balance modelling and CFD coupling
- Surrogate modelling and optimization for engineering systems
- Reproducible scientific computing for chemical and process engineering

## Selected Repositories

- [Introduction-to-Programming-in-OpenFOAM-12](https://github.com/SamMousaviES/Introduction-to-Programming-in-OpenFOAM-12): teaching-oriented OpenFOAM 12 examples covering custom models, solver structure, and implementation basics.
- [ScalarTransportFoam](https://github.com/SamMousaviES/ScalarTransportFoam): a custom OpenFOAM 12 solver that adds scalar transport capability for the PitzDaily case.
- [micromodelWaterAirInjection](https://github.com/SamMousaviES/micromodelWaterAirInjection): enhanced-oil-recovery micromodel simulations comparing water and air injection with `multiphaseInterFoam`.
- [of11_TrickleBed](https://github.com/SamMousaviES/of11_TrickleBed): OpenFOAM-based trickle-bed simulation work for multiphase reactor flow.
- [Karman-Vortex-Street](https://github.com/SamMousaviES/Karman-Vortex-Street): vortex shedding and flow-visualization work around a cylinder wake.
